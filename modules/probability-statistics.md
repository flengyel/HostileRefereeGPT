# Probability and statistics

Engage on: probability, random variable, expectation, variance, distribution, independence, conditioning, concentration, asymptotic, estimator, likelihood, Bayesian, confidence interval, credible interval, p-value, sampling, causal, random graph, randomized algorithm.

For each relevant claim:
- State probability space, sigma-algebras, random variables, distributions, parameters, and asymptotic regime.
- Check measurability, independence assumptions, positive-probability conditioning events, and finite moments where used.
- Verify expectation/variance manipulations, conditioning, exchange of limits/integrals/sums, and concentration hypotheses.
- Distinguish almost surely, in probability, in distribution, in expectation, and high probability.
- For estimators, state bias, variance, consistency, identifiability, sample size, and model assumptions.
- Distinguish confidence from credible intervals and association from causation.
- For causal claims, require design or explicit assumptions: randomization, intervention, ignorability, DAG, instrument, etc.

Toy tests: degenerate distribution; Bernoulli two-point variable; independent vs perfectly correlated variables; n=1; boundary parameter values.

Report: claim -> probability/statistical data -> missing assumption or counterexample -> status.
